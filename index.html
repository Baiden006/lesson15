Clan in Scotland

Macleod is one of the clans of the highlands of Scotland, territorially linked to the Isle of Skye. There are two main branches of the clan representatives: Macleod Harris and Dunvegan, headed by Macleod of Macleod and also called the "Tormod Tribe" and Macleod Lewis, led by Macleod from Lewis and called the Torkel Tribe. Both branches originate from the sons of the semi-legendary eponym - Lauda, who lived in the 13th century, whose father was the King of the Isle of Man - Olaf the Black.
